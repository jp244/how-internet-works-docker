What is the Internet?

The Internet came from an experiment called ARPANET (Advanced Research Projects Agency Network) that was a DoD research project. The thought was to build a communications system that would survive a nuclear attack and didnâ€™t have a single point of failure (centralized). The idea was to break larger pieces of data/messages into smaller packets and distribute them around the network to use the most efficient path to their intended destination (Distributed Packet-Switched Network).

Independent operators and providers of Internet access assume end-to-end connectivity and dissemination of information. These independent networks that make up the larger Internet as a whole are responsible for making sure traffic flows fairly and efficiently. This idea was monumental to the fundamental ideas of doing science (sharing information with others).
